About Me


I am an engineering graduate with a multidisciplinary background including electronics, embedded systems, and analytical chemistry.

I received a BS in electrical engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology in 2012 and MS in biological engineering from the University of Georgia in 2017.

My thesis work at the Skidaway Institute of Oceanography involved researching improvements in dissolved carbon analysis with higher precision, faster speeds, and more adaptable usage than currently available commercial instruments. This work resulted in a certificate for Coastal and Oceanographic Engineering.

Work experience includes Cisco Systems from 2013 to 2014 as a test engineer and the University of Georgia horticultural greenhouse as an electrical engineer in 2015.


I am an Atlanta native and currently live in the North Druid Hills neighborhood. During my free time I enjoy tinkering with gadgets, cooking, cycling, soccer, piano, guitar, and recently.. A lot of coding!
